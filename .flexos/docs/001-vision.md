# Vision

## The One-Line
An autonomous, local pipeline that downloads, transcribes, and forensically analyzes thousands of hours of election counting video overnight, surfacing democratic violations to human observers in real-time.

## The Problem
At 20:00 on election night in Bulgaria, over 12,000 polling stations close and begin counting, generating roughly 40,000 hours of video content. While this footage is made public for transparency, no human organization can watch it all overnight. Fraud, intimidation, and "corrected" protocols hide easily in the sheer volume of boring, static footage. Transparency without attention is useless.

## The Insight
Election fraud rarely happens in central Sofia; it happens in remote villages where local bosses exert pressure and independent monitors are rare. By leveraging local transcription models (`faster-whisper`) and headless reasoning APIs (Claude), a single consumer machine can prioritize and process these vulnerable sections first, finding the needles in the haystack without incurring massive cloud compute costs.

## Who It's For
**Primary User:** Election observers, investigative journalists, and civil society watchdogs working the night shift on election day. They are overwhelmed, tired, and need high-signal alerts, not more raw data.
**Explicitly NOT for:** The general public. This is a forensic tool, not a consumer social platform. It is not for casual browsing.

## What It Is
Nabludatel (Observer) is a command center for election integrity. It is an automated watchdog that works backward from the most vulnerable locations (villages and small towns). It downloads the footage, runs it through local voice-to-text models, deletes the heavy video to save disk space, and feeds the transcripts to a forensic AI. The AI listens for specific violations (intimidation, protocol tampering) and drops timestamped anomalies into a high-visibility triage dashboard for human verification.

## What It Is Not
- **Not a cloud SaaS platform:** It runs locally to avoid bandwidth bottlenecks and massive storage costs.
- **Not a video player:** It links directly to the official `evideo.bg` archive rather than hosting the video itself.
- **Not a definitive judge:** It flags anomalies for human verification; it does not automatically invalidate election results.

## Why Now
The availability of highly accurate, locally runnable transcription models (faster-whisper large-v3) and cost-effective, high-reasoning LLMs (Claude 3.5 Sonnet) makes it possible to process 40,000 hours of audio overnight on consumer hardware. Previous elections required thousands of human volunteers; today, one machine can do the initial triage.

## What Success Looks Like
- **Election Night (12 hours):** All 12,000 sections processed by 8:00 AM the morning after the election.
- **Day After (24 hours):** At least 10 verified, high-severity violations published by journalists using Nabludatel's exact timestamps and transcripts.

## Core Values Embedded in the Product
1. **Radical Transparency:** Every AI decision must include the exact transcript snippet and a direct link to the video timestamp.
2. **Protection of the Vulnerable:** The queue must always prioritize rural and small-town sections over major cities.
3. **Data Over Narrative:** The UI must be dense, objective, and clinical. No marketing fluff, just facts and timestamps.
4. **Resilience:** The system must recover gracefully from dropped internet connections or API timeouts without losing data.
