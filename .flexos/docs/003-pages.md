## Page Specifications

### 1. Command Dashboard (`/`)
- **Purpose:** The primary monitoring station. Provides a live feed of anomalies and system health.
- **Audience & arrival context:** A journalist or observer sitting in a dark room at 3:00 AM, drinking coffee, waiting for the system to find fraud. They are focused, tired, and need high-contrast, instantly readable information.
- **UX vision:** Bloomberg Terminal meets air traffic control. Dense, dark, monospace-heavy. No wasted space. The UI should feel urgent but completely objective.
- **Sections:**
  1. **Telemetry Sidebar (Left, fixed width):**
     - Contains: Brand header with a pulsing "live" indicator. Real-time metrics: Sections Queued, Processed, Critical Alerts, Disk Space Saved.
     - Why it's here: Reassures the user that the background pipeline is actually running and making progress.
  2. **Anomaly Feed (Right, fluid width):**
     - Contains: A reverse-chronological list of alert cards. Each card shows: Section ID, severity border (Red/Amber), AI title ("Intimidation Detected"), 2-line AI reasoning, and a monospace snippet of the transcript.
     - Why it's here: This is the core product output. It is the triage queue.
- **Key interactions:** Clicking an alert card opens the Section Detail overlay.
- **Empty/loading/error states:** 
  - Empty: "Pipeline active. 1,204 sections queued. No anomalies detected yet."
  - Error: If the SQLite read fails, show a stark red banner: "DATABASE CONNECTION LOST."
- **Conversion/action goal:** Get the user to click on a critical alert to verify the footage.

### 2. Section Detail (`/section/:id`)
- **Purpose:** Provide the full context needed to verify a single anomaly without losing sight of the main feed.
- **Audience & arrival context:** The user just saw a shocking AI alert and needs to know if it's real or a hallucination. They are skeptical and need evidence.
- **UX vision:** A slide-over panel (drawer) that covers the right half of the screen. It feels like opening a forensic dossier.
- **Sections:**
  1. **Header:** Section ID, Municipality, Village name, and Severity tag.
  2. **AI Reasoning Block:** The exact JSON output from Claude, rendered readably.
  3. **Contextual Transcript:** The 5 minutes of transcript before and after the flagged quote, highlighting the exact trigger phrase.
  4. **Action Bar (Sticky bottom):** A massive primary button: "Watch Video @ [Timestamp]". Secondary button: "Mark as False Positive".
- **Key interactions:** Clicking the primary button opens a new browser tab to `evideo.bg` at the specific timestamp.
- **Empty/loading/error states:** Loading skeleton while fetching the full transcript from SQLite.
- **Conversion/action goal:** User clicks to watch the source video.

### 3. Coverage Matrix (`/coverage`)
- **Purpose:** A macro-level view of the entire country's processing status.
- **Audience & arrival context:** The user wants to know "Are we done with Plovdiv yet?" or "Where are the most alerts coming from?"
- **UX vision:** A dense, sortable data table. Clinical and exhaustive.
- **Sections:**
  1. **Filter Bar:** Dropdowns for Region, Municipality, Status (Pending, Complete, Flagged).
  2. **The Matrix:** A virtualized list of all 12,000 sections. Columns: ID, Location, Rural/Urban, Status, Alert Count.
- **Key interactions:** Sorting by Alert Count to find hotspots. Clicking a row opens the Section Detail overlay.
- **Empty/loading/error states:** Standard table loading states.
- **Conversion/action goal:** Identify geographic patterns of anomalies.
